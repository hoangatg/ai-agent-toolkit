# Pricing Strategy

> Price is what you pay. Value is what you get. — Warren Buffett

---

## 1. Pricing Models

| Model | Best For | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| **Freemium** | High volume, low conversion | Free users cost money |
| **Free trial** | Complex products | Churn after trial |
| **Usage-based** | API, infrastructure | Revenue unpredictable |
| **Per-seat** | B2B, collaboration | Incentive to share seats |
| **Flat rate** | Simple products | Leaves money on table |
| **Tiered** | Most SaaS | Tier design complexity |

---

## 2. Tier Design

### Three-Tier Framework

| Tier | Purpose | Pricing Psychology |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| **Starter** | Get users in, build habit | Low friction entry |
| **Pro** | Core revenue driver | Best value (anchor) |
| **Enterprise** | Large accounts, custom | Contact sales |

### Feature Allocation

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Free = hook** | Core value, limited capacity |
| **Paid = power** | Unlock capacity, features |
| **Enterprise = control** | Admin, SSO, SLA, support |

---

## 3. Pricing Psychology

| Principle | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Anchoring** | Show expensive tier first |
| **Charm pricing** | $49 not $50 |
| **Annual discount** | "Save 20% with annual" |
| **Decoy effect** | Middle tier looks best value |
| **Social proof** | "Most popular" badge |

---

## 4. Value Metrics

### Choosing Your Value Metric

| Good Metric | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Aligns with customer value | They pay more as they get more |
| Easy to understand | No confusion about billing |
| Grows with usage | Revenue scales naturally |
| Predictable | Customers can forecast cost |

---

## 5. Pricing Page Design

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Comparison table** | Clear feature matrix |
| **Recommended tier** | Highlight with badge/color |
| **Annual toggle** | Show savings prominently |
| **FAQ** | Address objections |
| **Money-back guarantee** | Reduce risk |

---

## 6. Anti-Patterns

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|----------|-------|
| Copy competitor prices | Price based on your value |
| Too many tiers (5+) | 3-4 tiers maximum |
| Hide pricing | Be transparent |
| Never change prices | Review quarterly |
| Discount to close | Add value instead |

---

> **Remember:** Pricing is the most powerful lever for growth. A 1% improvement in pricing yields more revenue than 1% more customers.
